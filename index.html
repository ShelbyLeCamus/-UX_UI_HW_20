Readers are overwhelmed by the multitude of reading apps with questionable trustworthiness. How might we combine the best features from pre-existing apps and sources into a single, trustworthy and intelligent platform?
Bookish a mobile application that makes intelligent, personalized recommendations based on reader's preferences and a combined review from multiple trusted sources.
AdobeXD, Invision, Miro

Research

Objective: Understand how readers decide what book to read next with a focus on tools, sources, consumption habits, and friends' influence

Hypothesis: Readers use many sources (such as apps, websites and sponsored book clubs) to get book recommendations, but friends are the most trusted source

Key Findings

50% respondents said friends were their most trusted source.This indicated to us that recommendations needed to incorporate friends' opinions, but also include other sources. It needed to be a balance.

63% respondents said they look for titles monthly. This indicated to us that titles were looked for in chunks and it would need to be quick and easy to save books when searching.

Most important features in a book app were:
  • Getting personalized recommendations (70.4%)
  • Reading book reviews (57.4%)
  • Keeping track of the books you’ve read (57.4%)
